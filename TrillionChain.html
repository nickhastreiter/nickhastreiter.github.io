Market Cap

Market cap is the product of (market price per unit * total # of units). If there are 10,000,000 shares of a company and the last market trade was at $10, it's market cap is $100,000,000. Easy enough right?

Even if the price has been $0.01 for the last five years, if someone comes along and makes a purchase on the open market for $10, the market cap is $100,000,000.

That means if you've been holding 100,000 shares that have only been worth $1k for the last 5 years, and someone buys one share for $10 on open market, your shares are suddenly worth $1,000,000. How's it feel to be a millionaire?

Liquidity

Liquidity refers to the volume of units being traded. A market that moves a high amount of volume relative to it's total size can be considered to have a deep pool of liquidity. An easier way to think of it is how long it will take to have a buyer lined up and how much the order will affect the market price when the time comes to sell.
