Market Cap

Market cap is the product of (unit price * # of units). If there are 10,000,000 shares of a company and the last market trade was at $10, it's market cap is $100,000,000. Easy enough right?

Liquidity

Liquidity refers to the volume of units being traded. A market that moves a high amount of volume relative to it's total size can be considered to have a deep liquidity pool. An easier way to think of it is how long it will take to have a buyer lined up when the time comes to sell.
